Hi, my name is Doeun. Thanks for stopping by my corner of the internet.

Welcome to my classy HTML page which I know you minimalists out there appreciate.

I'm from Vancouver. I love to cold plunge early in the morning (in the rain), sip on homemade mushroom tea before my morning standup, squeeze in a 50K bike ride to Squamish during my lunch break (in my Hokas), and finally, nudge my heart rate up with a half-marathon before bed.. in the rain.. with no mattress.. on the hardwood floor to optimize my mindset.

For the longest time, I was a lifemaxxer. I wanted to be like Steve Jobs and build the next great thing. But by the time I wrapped up my sixth side project of that year, having made a few hundred bucks, I realized that if I'm gonna build things just to stay broke, I may as well build things that I find interesting.

I'm a programmer. I'm most proficient in TypeScript, and I make sure to dabble with Python and Go here and there to clout chase. I work at a small Vancouver-based company called LōD. We have a beautiful engineering culture, and it happens to be where I learned to appreciate true software craftsmanship.

A few times a year, I go camping with some of my best friends. In summer 2025, we went quite a bit, and even got to sneak in my first backpacking trip. While I never plan to go on another chill 7-hour, AllTrails 'hard', hiking trip (Deeks Lake) with a 100lb backpack, these are the memories I'll think back to on my deathbed.

I also sold my mountain bike that summer and bought a dead-simple single speed bike. It felt freeing to strip away the fancy stuff and just glide around the city on my bicycle knowing that if anything breaks, I know how to fix it.

The idea of stripping away the BS to focus on the core matter has become something of an obsession of mine. A friend encouraged me to try Emacs to help catalyze my downward spiral.

Perhaps it was my crippling phone/stimulus addiction that sparked this endeavour of enlightenment. I'm currently at war to win back my attention span. As a prisoner of the Apple Ecosystem, I don't have much freedom to dumb down my devices. But I somehow managed to delete the App Store and add a red tint to my screen that makes it look evil.

The results have been remarkable. I may still be a lifemaxxer, but I can now gym without listening to a podcast.

Thanks for reading. Say hi at bkwon38@gmail.com.
