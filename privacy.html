Doeun Kwon Information Privacy

A vast amount of personal data is generated through online activities. This data includes information on what individuals engage with, avoid, and repeat. The collection and analysis of this data have become increasingly sophisticated, thanks to advances in technology like AI. Data brokers can now aggregate information from various sources, including public records, browsing history, and private chats, to create comprehensive consumer profiles. These profiles are then used by businesses to influence individuals' choices and behaviors through targeted advertising and content recommendation systems. Personal data exists in multiple layers:

  1. Device Layer: Hardware devices, such as smartphones and computers, emit identifiers and sensor readings that can be tracked.
  2. Network Layer: Connection metadata, including IP addresses and search queries, is transmitted through ISPs and routers.
  3. Service Layer: Websites, apps, and cloud services collect usage logs, cookies, and interaction events.
  4. Data Layer: The content created or transmitted, such as messages, photos, and location traces, is stored in backups and caches.
  5. Profile Layer: Aggregated and inferred profiles are built from the lower layers, used for recommendation engines, advertising, and behavioral influence systems.
