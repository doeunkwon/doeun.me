Doeun Kwon Information Personal Data

Much of modern life now takes place online. Most online activity is extensively tracked by large technology companies. This tracking produces detailed behavioural data about individuals: what they attend to, engage with, avoid, and repeat.

People tend to underestimate the long-term significance of this accumulated behavioural data. Advances in technology and AI have greatly increased the usefulness of such data for predicting and shaping behaviour.

Data brokers are companies that buy and sell personal data. For example, credit reporting agencies buy personal data from data brokers and aggregate information from numerous sources, like public records and browsing history, to build comprehensive consumer profiles, like credit scores. They then sell this information to other businesses who are also interested in profiling people.

Content recommendation and advertising systems are also using behavioural data to influence what people see, think about, and buy. Advertising demonstrates that reliable influence over behaviour can be achieved without coercion or awareness.

If a system can influence how people are represented in the economy as well as their choices at scale, that influence functions as a form of control. As data collection and modelling technologies improve, the capacity for control increases. The result is a growing ability for those who control these systems to steer large groups of people towards their own desired outcomes.

Layers in which a digital footprint exists:

  1. Device Layer: The hardware you use (smartphones, computers, wearables, IoT devices) that emits identifiers, sensor readings, and locally stored files.
  2. Network Layer: The connection metadata (IP addresses, DNS queries) that traverses ISPs, routers, and proxies.
  3. Service Layer: Websites, apps, cloud services, and APIs that collect usage logs, cookies, authentication tokens, and interaction events.
  4. Data Layer: The actual content you create or transmit (messages, photos, documents, location traces) and the backups/caches where that content is stored.
  5. Profile Layer: Aggregated and inferred profiles built from the lower layers, used for recommendation engines, advertising, and behavioural‑influence systems.
